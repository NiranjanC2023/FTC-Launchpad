Mechanical Parts Guide

Overview of essential mechanical components used in FTC robot construction.

Structural Components

Aluminum Extrusion

The backbone of most FTC robots. Available in various profiles (1x1, 1x2, 2x2 inches) from vendors like REV Robotics and gobilda. Lightweight and easy to modify with tapped holes.

Brackets & Connectors

Metal brackets attach extrusion to motors, servo, and other components. Includes 90-degree brackets, T-slots, and custom fabricated pieces.

Fasteners

Bolts, screws, and nuts hold everything together. Common sizes include M3, M4, and M5. Use lock washers to prevent loosening during competition.

Motion Components

Motors

DC Motors: REV HD Hex, Core Hex, gobilda motors. Drive wheels and mechanisms.

Servo Motors: REV Smart Robot Servo. Precise angular positioning for mechanisms.

Gearboxes

Modify motor output using different gear ratios. Higher ratios = more torque, lower ratios = higher speed.

Wheels

Traction Wheels: Rubber grip for high-traction drivetrain

Mecanum Wheels: Omni-directional movement

Omni Wheels: Free-rolling in sideways direction

Linear Motion

Lead Screws: Convert rotation to linear motion for slides and lifts

Linear Slides: REV Linear Slides for smooth, rigid extension mechanisms

Belts & Pulleys: Transfer power over distance

Sensor Components

  • Color Sensors: Detect colors, distance, and brightness
  • Distance Sensors: Time-of-flight sensors for obstacle detection
  • Touch Sensors: Digital switches for limit detection
  • Encoders: Track motor rotation for precise movement
  • IMU: Measure acceleration, rotation, and orientation
  • Webcam: For AprilTag or color detection in autonomous

Electrical Components

  • Battery: 11.1V 3S LiPo batteries provide 30A capacity
  • Power Switch: Safety disconnect between battery and robot
  • Connectors: XT30 for main power, JST for sensors
  • Wiring: 10-12 AWG for main power, smaller for sensors
  • LED Lights: For troubleshooting and signaling

Tools & Supplies

  • Hex key set (1.5mm - 5mm)
  • Screwdrivers (Phillips and flat-head)
  • Measuring tape and calipers
  • Wrench or socket set
  • Hot glue gun and glue sticks
  • Thread-lock compound
  • Lubricating oil for smooth motion
  • Cable management clips and velcro

Budget Considerations

Mechanism: $200-400

Electronics & Control: $150-300

Sensors: $100-200

Fasteners & Misc: $50-100
