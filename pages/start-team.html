Start an FTC Team

Step-by-step guide to founding your own FIRST Tech Challenge robotics team.

Step 1: Find a Sponsor Organization

FTC teams must be sponsored by a school, community organization, or non-profit.

Options:

  • School (most common - through science department)
  • Community centers or recreation programs
  • Boys & Girls Clubs
  • Libraries
  • Non-profit organizations

Action: Contact your school administrator or local organization about starting a robotics program.

Step 2: Get FIRST Involved

  • Visit firstinspires.org for official information
  • Register your team with FIRST (requires team number assignment)
  • Pay team registration fee (approximately $500-600)
  • Join your regional FTC community and competition schedule

Step 3: Recruit Team Members

Build a diverse team with various skills:

  • Programmers: Code developers for TeleOp and autonomous
  • Mechanical Engineers: CAD designers and builders
  • Electrical Specialists: Wiring and sensor integration
  • Business Team: Fundraising and outreach

Ideal Team Size: 8-15 students for balanced workload

Step 4: Find a Coach/Mentor

You'll need adult mentors to:

  • Supervise meetings and work sessions
  • Provide technical guidance
  • Handle administrative tasks and registration
  • Mentor students in engineering and teamwork

Coaches don't need to be robotics experts - willingness to learn is key!

Step 5: Secure Funding

Initial Budget Needed: $3,000-5,000 for first year

  • Robot parts and electronics: $1,200-2,000
  • Competition registration: $500-600
  • Tools and workspace: $300-500
  • Travel to competitions: $400-1,000

Funding Sources:

  • School budget allocation
  • Corporate sponsorships
  • Parent donations
  • Fundraising events
  • Grants and STEM initiatives

Step 6: Set Up a Workspace

You need space for:

  • Building and testing (workshop/makerspace)
  • Meetings and planning (classroom or meeting room)
  • Storage for parts and tools
  • Computer workstations for programming and CAD

Many teams use school workshops, gymnasiums, or community maker spaces.

Step 7: Get Equipment & Parts

Essentials:

  • REV Robotics Control Hub
  • DC Motors and Servos
  • Sensors (color, distance, touch)
  • Aluminum extrusion and fasteners
  • Tools (drill, saw, hex keys, wrenches)
  • Computers for programming and CAD

See our Mechanical Parts Guide for detailed recommendations.

Step 8: Learn & Prepare

  • Review FTC rules and game manual
  • Complete online training modules from FIRST
  • Use resources on this site to learn programming and mechanical skills
  • Watch YouTube tutorials and community examples
  • Attend local workshops and events

Step 9: Prepare for Your First Competition

  • Register for competitions in your region
  • Build and test your first robot
  • Develop basic TeleOp and autonomous code
  • Practice driving and strategy
  • Prepare team materials (engineering notebook, business presentation)

Timeline for First Year

  • June-July: Register team, recruit members, secure funding
  • August: Set up workspace, order parts and tools
  • September-December: Build robot, learn programming, attend first competitions
  • January-March: Continue competing, improve robot and code
  • April-May: Finals and state competitions

Common Challenges & Solutions

Challenge: Not enough funding

Solution: Start smaller, focus on basic drivetrain first, seek sponsorships

Challenge: No coach available

Solution: Reach out to parent volunteers, local engineers, or college students

Challenge: Technical knowledge gaps

Solution: Use online resources, attend workshops, partner with experienced teams

Challenge: Limited workspace

Solution: Share space with maker communities, partner with local businesses

Resources & Support

  • FIRST Official Site: firstinspires.org/robotics/ftc
  • Game Manual: Updated annually with rules and challenge details
  • Community Forums: Chief Delphi community for advice and tips
  • YouTube Channels: Build guides and programming tutorials
  • Local Leagues: Connect with nearby teams for mentorship
