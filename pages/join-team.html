Join an FTC Team

Learn how to find and join an existing FIRST Tech Challenge robotics team.

Why Join an FTC Team?

  • Learn Real Engineering: Design, build, and program actual robots
  • Teamwork Skills: Collaborate with peers toward a common goal
  • Problem-Solving: Face real engineering challenges
  • Competition Experience: Test your skills against other teams
  • College Opportunities: Great for college applications and scholarships
  • Networking: Connect with mentors and like-minded students
  • Fun & Excitement: Build amazing robots and compete!

Step 1: Find Teams in Your Area

Official FIRST Website

Visit firstinspires.org and use their team finder:

  • Search by location (city, state, zip code)
  • Filter by competition level
  • View team contact information

Check Your School

  • Ask your guidance counselor about robotics programs
  • Check school clubs and activities fair
  • Look for announcements in science department
  • Ask physics or engineering teachers

Other Sources

  • Local Communities: Ask at community centers or libraries
  • Social Media: Search for local FTC teams on Facebook or Instagram
  • Word of Mouth: Talk to friends who participate in robotics
  • Competition Events: Attend local competitions and network

Step 2: Learn About the Team

Before joining, find out:

  • Meeting Schedule: When and where do they meet?
  • Commitment Level: How many hours per week?
  • Experience Level: Beginner-friendly or advanced?
  • Team Culture: What are they known for?
  • Costs: Are there fees or fundraising requirements?
  • Team Size: How many members do they have?
  • Competition Level: Do they compete locally, regionally, or nationally?

Step 3: Reach Out to the Team

How to Contact

  • Email: Contact the team lead or coach
  • Phone: Call the school or organization
  • Social Media: Message them on Facebook or Instagram
  • In Person: Attend a team meeting or competition

What to Say

Express your interest in robotics and ask:

  • Are you accepting new members?
  • What should new members know?
  • When is the next meeting?
  • What roles are available?
  • Is there an application process?

Step 4: Attend a Meeting or Open House

  • Go to see the team in action
  • Meet current members and coaches
  • Ask questions about the program
  • Learn about their robot and technical process
  • Understand the time commitment
  • Meet the people you'll be working with

Step 5: Complete the Application Process

Most teams have a simple process:

  • Application Form: Basic information about you
  • Parent Consent: Permission slip if you're under 18
  • Commitment Agreement: Agreement to attend meetings
  • Safety Agreement: Workshop and tool safety rules
  • Fees: Some teams charge participation fees

What to Expect as a New Member

First Few Weeks

  • Team orientation and safety training
  • Introduction to tools and workspace
  • Learning about the team's workflow
  • Getting assigned to a sub-team or role
  • Friendly environment with supportive teammates

Typical Time Commitment

  • During Season: 5-15 hours per week
  • Before Competitions: Extra sessions for prep
  • Off-Season: Varies (maybe 2-5 hours per week)
  • Competitions are usually on weekends

Team Roles Available

  • Programmers: Code TeleOp and autonomous
  • Mechanical Builders: Build robot structure
  • Electrical Team: Wiring and sensors
  • Business/Outreach: Fundraising and community
  • Strategy: Planning game strategy

Tips for New Members

  • Start Small: You don't need prior experience
  • Ask Questions: Experienced members love helping
  • Be Reliable: Show up to meetings on time
  • Collaborate: Robotics is a team sport
  • Learn Continuously: Use resources on this site
  • Have Fun: Enjoy the experience and make friends
  • Embrace Failure: Robots break - that's how we learn

Costs of Joining a Team

  • Team Fee: $0-500 (varies by team)
  • Competition Travel: Often covered by team or shared
  • Uniform/Shirt: $10-30 (optional)
  • Equipment: Usually provided by team
  • Fundraising: May participate in team fundraisers

Note: Most teams understand financial constraints and work with students who can't pay full fees. Ask about scholarship opportunities!

Questions to Ask Teams

  • What's your team's vision and goals?
  • How often do we meet and where?
  • Do you have experience requirements?
  • How do you assign new members to roles?
  • What's your competition schedule?
  • How do you support learning for beginners?
  • What's your team culture like?
  • Are there opportunities to advance my skills?

If You Can't Find a Local Team

  • Start One: See our "Start a Team" guide
  • Online Resources: Watch tutorials to learn at your own pace
  • Local Maker Spaces: Some have robotics groups
  • Attend Competitions: Network with teams and learn
  • Virtual Teams: Some teams offer remote participation

Key Takeaways

  • Use FIRST's website to find teams near you
  • Attend meetings to see if the team is right for you
  • No prior experience needed - teams welcome beginners
  • Be ready to commit time and effort
  • Ask questions and connect with team members
  • Have fun learning and collaborating!
